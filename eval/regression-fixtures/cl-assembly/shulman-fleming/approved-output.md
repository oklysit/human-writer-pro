The difficult part of cybersecurity is that it's asymmetric. An attacker only has to succeed once, and with AI the data they can access on that one compromise is an order of magnitude greater than it was before. AI does its best work when verifiability is the bottleneck: a directory traversal, a popped shell, indicators of compromise it can latch onto and iterate until the result is achieved. Defending is much more difficult because you have to analyze entire systems, see the way that they work together, and you can't be wrong once. It really is a risk management practice because it's not as verifiable; we can't say yes we have secured the system, we can only say no based on risk analysis, this is the best bang for our buck. Grounding is how AI helps defenders reclaim ground: in my AI study tool every answer traces back to a specific passage, so trust is click-verifiable. The same pattern applies to automated alert systems in SIEMs and log analysis.
