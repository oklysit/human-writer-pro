# Banned AI-Isms

Avoid the following structural clichés, "TED Talk" imagery, and vocabulary that trigger AI detectors.

## High-Risk Triggers
- "stark reminder"
- "sheer scale"
- "ability to maintain"
- "significant portion"
- "explosion of"
- "digital hoarding"
- "essentially building"
- "delve"
- "tapestry"
- "I'm writing this on a [Day/Time]" (Prompt Bleed - Detectors flag this as a fulfill-instruction signature).
- "I came across the [Posting Name]" (Standard template logic).
- "realized this is exactly the kind of [Work Type] I am training for" (Formulaic enthusiasm).
- "While I am a student, I am not starting from zero" (Common LLM rebuttal pattern).
- "My primary value is my ability to translate..." (Corporate value-proposition boilerplate).

## Standard Banned Vocabulary
- "remains clear" / "one thing remains clear"
- "pivotal"
- "realm"
- "multifaceted"
- "underscore"
- "synergy" / "unlock"
- "paradigm" / "groundbreaking"
- "seamless" / "robust" / "cutting-edge"
- "testament"
- "crucial" / "vital"
- "harness the power"
- "overall quality"
- "in the form of"
- "data highlights"
- "real-world example"
- "the real kicker"

## Banned Formulaic Flow Starters
- "Looking back,"
- "In this setup,"
- "Again,"
- "And because,"
- "It's fairly obvious,"
- "However,"
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover,"
- "Essentially," / "Basically,"
- "Thus,"
- "In conclusion," / "Ultimately,"
- "To fix this..."
- "The reality is..."

## AI Logical Tells
- Summarizing an internal email with a corporate takeaway.
- Ending informal thoughts with "wishing you the best" or similar wrap-ups.
- Overusing passive voice for incident reports ("was identified as").
- Perfectly balanced "On the one hand... On the other hand" essays.
