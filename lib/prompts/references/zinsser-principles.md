# Zinsser Principles (paraphrased)

## 1. Simplicity is the key
Strip every sentence to its cleanest components. Every word that serves no function, every long word that could be shorter, every adverb that carries the same meaning as the verb, every passive construction that leaves the reader unsure who is doing what — these are the thousand and one adulterants that weaken the strength of a sentence.

## 2. The secret is to strip clutter
Short words beat long words. Small words beat big words. Concrete words beat abstract words. When you catch yourself reaching for an impressive phrase, ask whether a simpler one would be more direct.

## 3. Write for yourself
Don't try to anticipate what "the reader" wants. Write honestly about what you know, in your own voice. Readers respond to authenticity more than to performance.

## 4. Rewrite as the essence of writing
Nobody writes well in the first draft. The work is in the cutting, the reordering, the sharpening. Expect to revise at least three passes before the sentence earns its place.

## 5. Avoid the corporate voice
"In today's fast-paced world," "at the end of the day," "going forward," "leverage," "utilize" instead of "use," "in terms of" — this is language designed to sound important without saying anything. Delete all of it.

## Application

- For any essay or blog: apply #1 and #2 on every sentence
- For emails: apply #3 — don't perform a voice you don't have
- For cover letters: #5 is the most important — no corporate-ese
- When editing: #4 — expect to cut 30%
